About

My Story

In 2021, armed with all the knowledge necessary for a career in higher academia, I graduated university with a double-degree in Political Science and History, and a minor in Global Asian Studies. However, in the midst of early mid-life crisis, I realized that higher academia just wasn’t for me.

I realized that I didn’t want just record experiences, I wanted to help create them for everyday people. I wanted to express my creativity, and utilize the soft skills that my degree taught me in a way that impacted peoples’ daily lives. I wanted to give people the tools and help that they had been deprived of, just because their community wasn’t well-represented. And so, turning my back on academia (but still holding onto all the valuable lessons and values it taught me), I decided to enroll in CareerFoundry’s UX Design program.

Now, I aim to create designs that are accessible and inclusive to all backgrounds, as well as adaptive to the needs of all my users. That means, I intend on always going the extra mile for any project I undertake. While I’m flexible and easy-going, I am also always on the search to understand how to make a product or experience even better.
